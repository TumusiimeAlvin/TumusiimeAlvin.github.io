Life of a Starfish

Starfish, also known as sea stars, are one of the most fascinating creatures in the ocean. Despite their name, they are not actually fish at all. They belong to a group of marine animals called echinoderms, which also includes sea urchins and sea cucumbers. There are over 2,000 known species of starfish found in oceans all around the world, from shallow coastal waters to the deep sea floor.

Facts About Starfish

  • Starfish are not actually fish — they are echinoderms, more closely related to sea urchins.
  • Starfish can regenerate lost arms and even entire bodies from a single arm.
  • Starfish have no brain or blood; they rely on a water vascular system for movement and feeding.
  • There are over 2,000 known species of starfish found in oceans around the world.
